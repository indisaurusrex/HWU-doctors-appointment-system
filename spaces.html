Main Hall Studios

Prices (these are very basic and are subject to change while we develop our offer) : Small studios ( main hall) - £300 per month

This area is designed to provide open plan work spaces which can be visible to the public such as jewelers or painters showcasing their practice to the public as well as providing direct access to sales of their products through display cabinets and wall space (green), the current commission charge for sales is set at 25% which shows exceptional value to the maker. Until 2017 Stour Space operated a ‘SHOP’ in it’s premises selling the work of over 300 local artists generating economic and marketing support and representation. The SHOP was forced to close through the impact of local developments and since then there has been no similar support. Building on our past experience and longstanding connections with the arts community the shop in this premises will continue to support recovery from covid over the next year.

Through a curated process other makers in the area will be able to showcase their work for sale and exposure

Residents will further be supported to give workshops through regular ‘training days’ where social distancing supports this and will be trained in how to manage this to build their capacity.

Classroom

Enquire for prices

Classroom (+300sqft) will be a clean, flexible space of which will used primarily as education space and Civic classroom - MA students year 1 and 2 - Design for Cultural Commons (London Metropolitan University) will operate from this site including direct support via a bursary to two students who will work alongside the organisation in a paid position and producing real time monitoring research. A program of Civic education will operate from November in partnerships with Civic State, School for Civic Action, London Met, Design Museum and the Tate Exchange and Innovate UK.

The room will be fitted out with projectors, audiovisual and photographic equipment to facilitate:

  • Lectures, workshops and training space available on free or favourable terms.
  • Podcast studio
  • Photographic studio for resident product marketing
  • Meeting space (in line with social distancing)
  • Operational space to support management of premises.

Forecourt

The forecourt can be used as flexible space for markets, food provision and expanded public space to support social distancing. Currently discussions are being had with a mobile package free grocery store which through much consultation is something highly requested by the community.

Future partnerships are currently in development for modular buildings to support sustainability programs, training and interim use workspace as the needs of the area change with plans to relocate these locally in the future.

Collaborations with Wick CNC, a local partner company developed with Hackney Council and the GLA will be used to manufacture these building, employing local people and supporting a local circular economy.
