Hackney Wick Underground

Hackney Wick Underground is a grassroots nonprofit organisation led by Civic State. . A live project that practices commons principles, acknowledging the ideology as a valuable and inclusive means of governing space. We are as much a workspace as a collective, exhibition space and a community, offering affordable studio space along with a host of open source provisions at reasonable rates for the residents of Hackney Wick and Fish Island.

“Designing a collective workspace based on commons principles reintroduces collective orchestration while creating opportunities for learning and knowledge sharing between community members. We are opening up right to use land for community members to organise and learn together, these values are central to this business model.”
- Neil McDonald, Director.

While the primary objective for this project is based around affordable creative workspace, the scope for it’s impact is far wider providing significant research and development to be applied to the area and further afield in line with many local and london wide initiatives including the Mayor of London’s Cultura at Risk team, HWFI Creative Enterprise Zone (CEZ) and in collaboration with: Stour Space, London Metropolitan University, UCL, Loughborough University, Innovate UK ,HWFI Community Development Trust (CDT) and a number of local partners and international committees. The project aims to demonstrate new models of business, place-making and civic engagement, it will reflect and showcase community heritage, culture and diversity, support the creation of approximately 20 new jobs, develop skills and encourage socio-economic resilience through: capacity building, workshops, training and education, support the wellbeing of people through community cohesion, civic engagement and empower people in their capacity as citizens to be active members of their community.

The project draws on over a decade of empirical research and development through the creation and development of Stour Space as well as the HWFI CDT in this locality and thus is uniquely placed to address Creative Enterprise Zone (CEZ) priorities and that of LLDC, LBTH and LBH and the GLA of which there has been a long term relationship.a

Mateusz Kotecki

A photographer using visual media to connect with the social issues The main focus of my creative activities is based on visual media being photography & film . I have received a MA degree in Photography from Middlesex University. I have created 3 books, 2 of each have been focused on small artists in need of help. 1st one focused on the artist struggling to make it in the industry and having to support themselves via hospitality work. The project was based on portraying the artist trying to create their own work in the self made studios in their home. 2nd was a street project on buskers & street artist that choose to take their craft to the public rather then being constricted by venues, galleries and such.

Since the ending of my course in 2018 I have worked with a number of artists and organisations & became a member of several arts collectives.

Over the last year I got involved with an organisation with which on join forces we’ve taken and abandon space and turned it into a artist creative hub.

On top of managing that site, I am personally working on a documentary for the organisation as well as offering free product photography and visual content for the artist involved to support then during those trying times.
